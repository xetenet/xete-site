Privacy

We built xete so that we can't read your data.

Messages are encrypted on your device to their recipient; our server relays ciphertext it cannot open.

Effective: July 2026 · Operated by XETENET LLC, an Oregon limited liability company · Contact: xetenet@gmail.com

The short version

xete is built so that we can't read your data. Messages are encrypted on your device to their recipient; our server relays ciphertext it cannot open. On Saga and Seeker, your keys live in your phone's hardware Seed Vault and never leave it. We don't run ads, we don't sell data, and we collect as little as the service can run on. xete is not a service for meeting or contacting strangers — you reach people you already know, and by default only people you've connected with can message you.

What we can see, and what we can't

We cannot see: the content of your messages, your private keys, your vault files, or the contents of private contact cards. All of it is encrypted on your device with keys we never hold. (Attachments are a planned feature, not live yet — this same protection will apply once they ship.)

This means we also cannot moderate, filter, or scan message content — there is nothing for us to scan. If someone sends or receives something abusive, you can block that sender and report the account (not its content, which we can't read) to us at xetenet@gmail.com. We act on reports at the account level and cooperate with lawful law enforcement requests. We're telling you this plainly rather than implying a protection we can't actually provide.

We do handle: the minimum needed to relay and protect the service —

  • Ciphertext in transit and at rest. Message content is deleted from our server within 24 hours of delivery (longer for paid tiers) — enforced automatically, not just a policy on paper. A delivery record (sender and recipient identifiers, timestamps, size) currently persists longer for anti-abuse and reliability; we are working to shorten that too.
  • Your public identity. Your wallet address, your messaging public key, and any %alias you claim are all public by design — they live on Solana or in the on-chain alias registry, the same way a username is public on any platform.
  • Anti-abuse signals. IP addresses are not stored as part of normal registration. They may be temporarily used only while actively responding to abuse or rate-limit violations — never for advertising or profiling, never sold, never shared except as the law compels.
  • On-device diagnostics. The app keeps a local, on-device log of technical events (errors, connection status) to help you troubleshoot — never message content, keys, or passwords. It never leaves your device unless you explicitly export and send it yourself (e.g., attaching it to a support request).

What happens on-chain

%alias claims, trades on the Counter, swaps, and settlements are Solana transactions: public, permanent, and outside anyone's ability to edit or delete — including ours. Everything else stays off the chain on purpose. The chain sees only what must settle.

Your keys, your control

On Saga and Seeker, keys are generated and held in your device's hardware Seed Vault secure element and never leave it. On other devices, xete falls back to on-device software key storage, which does not have the same hardware-backed guarantee. Either way, we cannot recover your keys, freeze them, or act for you. Deleting the app removes its on-device data; your on-chain records (aliases, trades) remain, as with any blockchain.

Your rights

You can ask us (xetenet@gmail.com) what we hold about your account and request deletion of anything we can delete server-side — registration and delivery-record data, not already-expired message content (which is gone automatically) and not on-chain records (which no one, including us, can delete — see above).

Third parties

Price quotes and routing use Jupiter's public API; on-chain reads use Helius. These services see the queries needed to serve you (e.g., token pairs, wallet addresses being looked up) but never your keys or message content.

Children

xete is not directed at, and may not be used by, anyone under 13. When you set up xete, you'll be asked to confirm you meet this minimum age before you can continue — this confirmation happens on your device and is never sent anywhere or stored by us.
